1. Mike Rozier

Mike Rozier was a historical running back for Nebraska from 1981 to 1983, allowing the Huskers to go 33-5 during his time there. He only played three seasons, but Rozier is the all-time leading rusher at Nebraska. He managed to rack up 4,780 rushing yards while only playing 35 games, an average of 136 yards per game. Rozier has the record for most 100-yard rushing games (26), most 200-yard rushing games (7), the most touchdowns (52), and most points by a single player (312). When Rozier was a senior in 1983, he had his best season yet. He was the consensus National Player of the Year, a first-team All-American, and won the Heisman Trophy (best player in college football). Rozier ran for 2,148 yards in his senior season. At the time, that was the second-highest total in NCAA history.

2. Tommie Frazier

Tommie grew up in Palmetto, Florida. He was just an 18-year-old true freshman when he started playing for Nebraska in 1982. Tommie Frazier played football at Nebraska from 1982-1985. His overall record at Nebraska was 45-4, with 5,476 total yards on offense and 79 touchdowns. His jersey number was retired in 1966. According to Sports Magazine, he is listed as one of the greatest college football players of the century. In 1994, he was the pre-season favorite to win the Heisman. At the start of this season, he had started 23 consecutive games with a 20-3 record, competing in two Orange Bowls. After starting out 4-0, he was sidelined due to issues with blood clots in his leg. He bounced back in 1995 fully healthy again. He led Nebraska to a National Championship, defeating the Florida Gators. He rushed for 199 yards and scored two touchdowns. He is well known for his incredible 75-yard touchdown run, where he bounced off multiple defenders. Many people believe he got snubbed from a Heisman in 1995, losing it to Eddie George out of Ohio State. He now hosts Tommie Fraziers Xs and Os on touchdowntommie.com
