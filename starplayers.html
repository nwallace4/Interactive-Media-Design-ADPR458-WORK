1. Mike Rozier

Mike Rozier was a historical running back for Nebraska from 1981 to 1983, allowing the Huskers to go 33-5 during his time there. He only played three seasons, but Rozier is the all-time leading rusher at Nebraska. He managed to rack up 4,780 rushing yards while only playing 35 games, an average of 136 yards per game. Rozier has the record for most 100-yard rushing games (26), most 200-yard rushing games (7), the most touchdowns (52), and most points by a single player (312). When Rozier was a senior in 1983, he had his best season yet. He was the consensus National Player of the Year, a first-team All-American, and won the Heisman Trophy (best player in college football). Rozier ran for 2,148 yards in his senior season. At the time, that was the second-highest total in NCAA history.

2. Tommie Frazier

Tommie grew up in Palmetto, Florida. He was just an 18-year-old true freshman when he started playing for Nebraska in 1982. Tommie Frazier played football at Nebraska from 1982 to 1985. His overall record at Nebraska was 45-4, with 5,476 total yards on offense and 79 touchdowns. His jersey number was retired in 1966. According to Sports Magazine, he is listed as one of the greatest college football players of the century. In 1994, he was the pre-season favorite to win the Heisman. At the start of this season, he had started 23 consecutive games with a 20-3 record, competing in two Orange Bowls. After starting 4-0, he was sidelined due to issues with blood clots in his leg. He bounced back in 1995 fully healthy again. He led Nebraska to a National Championship, defeating the Florida Gators. He rushed for 199 yards and scored two touchdowns. He is well known for his incredible 75-yard touchdown run, where he bounced off multiple defenders. Many people believe he got snubbed from a Heisman in 1995, losing it to Eddie George out of Ohio State. He now hosts Tommie Fraziers Xs and Os on touchdowntommie.com

3. Ndamukong Suh

Ndamukong Suh is from Portland, Oregon, where he attended Grant High School. For the first two years of his career, he battled injuries (knee surgery) and was a backup. In 2007, he played in all 12 games and managed to rack up 34 tackles on the season. In his Junior season, he was the first defensive lineman to lead the team in tackles since 1973. His 76 tackles on the season were the most by a Husker lineman since 1992. Although he was making an incredible amount of tackles, he also managed to intercept two passes and return them for touchdowns. He finished the season having nine games where he collected five or more tackles. In his Senior Season, he had one of the best years in college football history for a defensive lineman. He was the first defensive player Associated Press College Player of the Year Award, the first defensive tackle invited to the Heisman ceremony since 1994, and won the Outland Trophy for the nation's top interior lineman. Having 85 tackles, he became the first defensive lineman to lead Nebraska in tackles in consecutive seasons. His 12 sacks on the season rank third in school history. In the 2010 NFL draft, he was selected second overall by the Detroit Lions.

4. Johnny Rodgers

Johnny Rodgers was born and raised in Omaha, Nebraska. He attended Omaha Tech High, which closed in 1984. He was so fast his well-known nickname was Johnny "The Jet" Rodgers. From 1970 to 1972, he was a running back, punt returner, and sometimes a wide receiver at Nebraska. Rodgers broke nearly every offensive team record at Nebraska. He won the Heisman trophy in 1972, was named to the College Football All-American team twice, and won the Walter Camp Award. He established a record with the NCAA, rushing for 5,586 all-purpose yards. His greatest single performance took place in the 1973 Orange Bowl. He led his team to a 40-6 victory over Notre Dame while having three rushing touchdowns, caught a 50-yard pass for a touchdown, and threw a 54-yard touchdown pass. He did all this while leaving the game when there were still 21 minutes left on the clock. He was selected as the 25th pick in the first round of the NFL draft in 1973. He decided to play in the Canadian Football League for the Montreal Alouettes.

5. Eric Crouch

Eric Crouch is from Omaha, Nebraska. He attended Millard North High School. Crouch had a promising career early, but his best year was his senior year in 2001. He won the Heisman Trophy as well as the Davey O'Brien National Quarterback Award. He completed 105 out of 189 passes for 1,510 yards in seven resulting touchdowns. He also rushed for 1,115 yards having 18 touchdowns. He also appeared on the cover of the video game "NCAA College Football 2k3". He came up short of winning the National Championship, losing to the Miami Hurricanes 37-14 in the Rose Bowl.
